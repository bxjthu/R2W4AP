Shooting the breeze

The 3D shapes of many plant seeds are optimized to aid dispersion by wind â€” often allowing the seeds to travel hundreds of kilometres .

Taking inspiration from these aerial hitchhikers , John Rogers and his colleagues in this issue present a series of 3D macro- , meso - and microscale fliers that can similarly use wind for dispersal .

The researchers were able to maintain flight times even when the fliers were weighed down with active electronic payloads .

They note that one example application for the fliers could be as battery - free , wireless devices for atmospheric measurements .
